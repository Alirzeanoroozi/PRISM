
1) count_pairs.py
- Purpose: Counts how many unique pairs from merged.txt have any results in rosetta_output_1.
- Logic: Reads merged.txt, normalizes pairs (order-independent), scans rosetta output filenames,
  and reports matched vs missing counts.
- Use when: You only need the overall hit/miss counts.

2) count_pairs_detailed.py
- Purpose: Produces a detailed job-wise breakdown and a full text report.
- Logic: Tracks which joblist folders contain each matched pair, then summarizes top/bottom jobs.
- Output: pair_analysis_report.txt with top/bottom jobs, full job list, and missing pairs list.
- Use when: You want a full text report with job-wise details and missing pairs.

3) generate_csv_reports.py
- Purpose: Converts the analysis into CSV files for spreadsheet use.
- Output CSVs:
  - overall_summary.csv: high-level totals and percentages.
  - job_wise_summary.csv: pairs found per joblist + percent of total.
  - pairs_with_results.csv: each matched pair and the joblist(s) where it appears.
  - pairs_without_results.csv: list of pairs with no results found.
- Use when: You want the data in CSV format for filtering/plots.

Results (insight):

- overall_summary.csv: Shows total coverage. Current run: 231/451 pairs (51.2%) have results.
- job_wise_summary.csv: Highlights strongest/weakest joblists by pairs found. Useful to spot
  joblists that produced fewer outputs than expected.
- pairs_with_results.csv: Confirms which pairs produced results and where they were found.
- pairs_without_results.csv: Primary list of missing pairs to investigate or re-run.

Notes:
- Pair normalization is order-independent, so swapped names (e.g., A_B vs B_A) are treated
  as the same pair.
- Counts are based on filename patterns: *_PDB1_0_PDB2_0.rosetta_* .
